# Sunshine — Landing Page Plan

---

## 1. What Is the Goal?

**One goal: get people to book into a live event.**

That's it. Every section of the page exists to support that single conversion. The visitor arrives from a Facebook/Instagram ad that already told them to "book into the event" — so the landing page must make it effortless to do exactly that, while giving them enough confidence in the product to follow through.

The page is NOT trying to sell an apartment. It's selling the *next step* — showing up in person.

---

## 2. What Content Should the Page Include?

The content falls into two categories:

### A. Primary Content — The Event (drives the conversion)
- **Three event date/time options** with a booking CTA for each
- **Urgency/scarcity indicators** — spots remaining per event
- **What to expect at the event** — see luxury finishes, experience the ocean views, meet the developer
- **Final event badge** on 26 March — tasteful FOMO, not aggressive

### B. Supporting Content — The Apartments (builds desire while they scroll)
- **Hero intro** — "Downsize but not downgrade", four luxury beachside apartments
- **The vision** — architectural concept, Invilla collaboration, wave-form design
- **Curated interiors** — living, kitchen, master suite, outdoor/rooftop highlights
- **Location & lifestyle** — Sunshine Beach, Noosa, walkability, dining & recreation
- **Key specs** — 4 bed, 3.5 bath, private pool, private lift, rooftop, ~367–405 m²
- **Premium inclusions** — Miele appliances, travertine, home automation, EV charging
- **Timeline** — move-in ready by mid-2026
- **Only 4 residences** — scarcity reinforcement

---

## 3. What Order & Structure?

The structure follows the visitor's journey: **arrive → see CTA → scroll for confidence → book.**

| # | Section | Purpose |
|---|---------|---------|
| **1** | **Hero + Event Booking** | Immediate CTA. Three event cards with dates, times, spots remaining, and "Book Now" buttons. Headline like *"Experience Sunshine — In Person"*. This is above the fold. |
| **2** | **What to Expect** | Short section: "See the luxury finishes first hand, experience the ocean views, and meet the developer." Bridges the gap between clicking CTA and scrolling further. |
| **3** | **The Vision** | Architectural story — Invilla, wave-form design, light & views. Sets the tone of quality. Pull from Chapter 1 of brochure. |
| **4** | **Curated Interiors** | Visual highlight cards — Living, Kitchen, Master Suite, Outdoor/Rooftop. Pull from Chapter 2. Makes them *want* to see it in person. |
| **5** | **Location & Lifestyle** | Sunshine Beach, Noosa, walking distance to beach & village, dining, recreation. Pull from Chapters 3 & 4. Sells the lifestyle, not just the apartment. |
| **6** | **At a Glance** | Quick-scan specs: 4 bed / 3.5 bath / pool / lift / rooftop / ~400 m² / mid-2026 move-in / only 4 available. |
| **7** | **Repeat Event CTA** | Mirror of the hero booking section. They've now seen enough — make it easy to book without scrolling back up. |
| **8** | **Footer** | Logo, legal disclaimer, credits. |

### Key Principle
> Sections 1–2 handle the visitor who's ready to book immediately.  
> Sections 3–6 handle the visitor who needs more convincing.  
> Section 7 catches them at the bottom so they don't have to scroll back up.

## Content Style

The page has two voices that need to work together:

**The brochure** is poetic and editorial — *"nestled amongst the textured coastal canvas"*. Beautiful, but too slow for a landing page where you have seconds to convert.

**The video ad** is direct and personal — *"downsize but not downgrade"*, *"spots are limited"*. Punchy, warm, and action-oriented.

**The landing page should sit between the two:**

| Aspect | Approach |
|--------|----------|
| **Tone** | Confident and warm, not salesy. Premium without being pretentious. |
| **Copy length** | Short and scannable. No long paragraphs — use headlines, bullet points, and pull quotes. |
| **Headings** | Direct and benefit-led. *"Private pool. Private lift. Private rooftop."* rather than *"Reimagining residential living."* |
| **Body text** | Adapted from the brochure but trimmed to 1–2 sentences max per section. Let images do the heavy lifting. |
| **Event section** | Most direct — clear, simple, action-oriented. *"Choose your session. Spots are limited."* |
| **Product sections** | Slightly more editorial — draws from brochure language but stays concise. |
| **CTAs** | Warm but clear: *"Reserve Your Spot"*, *"Book Now"* — not *"Enquire"* or *"Learn More"* |
| **Scarcity language** | Factual, not pushy: *"10 spots remaining"*, *"Final event"*, *"Only 4 residences"* |

**The overall feel:** Like a beautifully designed invite from someone you trust — not a hard sell.
